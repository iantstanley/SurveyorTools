About Elevation Certificates

FEMA Elevation Certificates document a property’s First Floor Height (FFH) and adjacent elevation, essential for flood risk assessment under the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP).

How to Obtain One

Contact local floodplain managers, hire licensed surveyors/engineers/architects, or self-certify in some cases.

Why They Matter

They determine flood risk, ensure regulatory compliance, and can influence insurance rates or flood zone status.

Tips for Property Owners

Keep certificates current, especially after modifications. Understand FFH and consider elevating structures to reduce risk and insurance costs.
